I am a PhD student in Mathematics at the University of Sussex, working under the supervision of Dr. Antoine Dahlqvist.

My research lies at the interface of mathematical physics, probability, geometry, and representation theory. I am especially interested in two-dimensional Yang--Mills theory, large-N limits, Wilson loop observables, Makeenko--Migdal equations, random matrix theory, and moduli spaces of flat connections.

Current projects

Yang--Mills measure and the master field on the real projective plane

This project studies the large-N limit of two-dimensional Yang--Mills theory on the real projective plane. The main goal is to understand the master field for Wilson loop observables on a basic non-orientable surface.

A central feature is the distinction between contractible and non-contractible loops. Contractible loops can be compared with their lifts to the two-sphere, while non-contractible loops detect the topology of the projective plane. The project uses heat-kernel Yang--Mills measures, Wilson loop observables, representation-theoretic estimates, and Makeenko--Migdal equations to study the large-N behaviour.

More broadly, this work is part of a programme to extend rigorous master-field results from orientable surfaces to non-orientable surfaces.

Atiyah--Bott--Goldman measure and the low-area limit of two-dimensional quantum Yang--Mills measure

This project studies the small-area limit of two-dimensional Yang--Mills measure on a compact oriented surface. As the total area tends to zero, the heat kernels in the lattice Yang--Mills measure force the curvature to vanish, so the measure concentrates on the moduli space of flat connections.

The aim is to identify the limiting measure on the character variety as the normalized Atiyah--Bott--Goldman Liouville measure. The proof is organized around the relation between two structures: Goldman's Poisson bracket for Wilson loop functions on the character variety, and the Makeenko--Migdal equations for lattice Yang--Mills theory.
