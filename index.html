I am a PhD student in Mathematics at the University of Sussex, working under the supervision of Dr. Antoine Dahlqvist.

My research lies at the interface of mathematical physics, probability, geometry, and representation theory. I am especially interested in two-dimensional Yang--Mills theory, large-N limits, Wilson loop observables, Makeenko--Migdal equations, random matrix theory, and moduli spaces of flat connections.

Current projects

Large-N Yang--Mills theory on non-orientable surfaces

This project studies the large-N limit of two-dimensional quantum Yang--Mills theory, with particular focus on non-orientable surfaces. The goal is to understand how topology and orientability affect Wilson loop observables and their deterministic master-field limits.

The work combines heat-kernel Yang--Mills measures, random matrix theory, free probability, representation theory, and Makeenko--Migdal equations. A central theme is to compare the behaviour of contractible and non-contractible loops and to understand which parts of the orientable theory survive in the non-orientable setting.

Wilson loops, character varieties, and symplectic geometry

This project concerns the geometric structure behind Wilson loop observables on moduli spaces of flat connections. The main objects are character varieties, Goldman-type Poisson brackets, symplectic forms, and natural measures on spaces of representations.

One motivation is to relate identities coming from two-dimensional Yang--Mills theory to the symplectic and Poisson geometry of character varieties. In this direction, Wilson loops can be viewed both as observables in gauge theory and as functions on moduli spaces of flat connections.
